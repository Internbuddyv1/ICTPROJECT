Recruitment Interview – Windrush Descendant Candidate
Experience a hiring panel from multiple perspectives where immigration history and assumptions about “fitting in” shape the conversation.
Duration
15 mins
Perspectives
3
You will switch between the candidate, the hiring manager, and HR. Notice how references to “culture fit” and “professionalism” land differently depending on whose viewpoint you are in.
Current Scene

You are sitting in the waiting area of a City firm. This is your second-round interview for an analyst role. Your family migrated to the UK as part of the Windrush generation, and you are the first in your family to work in finance.

Through the open meeting-room door, you overhear two panel members. One says quietly, “We need someone who really fits the culture here. People who don’t ‘get’ the way things work can struggle, even if they’re technically strong.”

How do you respond?
  • A) Ignore the comment and focus on your upcoming interview.
  • B) Make a mental note and later ask HR about how “culture fit” is defined.
  • C) Gently challenge the assumption in the interview by highlighting how different backgrounds have helped you navigate complex situations.
