This Culture Readiness Report is generated from anonymised, mock data collected through immersive scenarios in the Trading Places prototype. It is designed for demonstration and teaching purposes only and does not represent a real organisation.
Key Strengths
1. High completion of mandatory training
72% of staff have completed the full DEI training pathway, indicating strong engagement with the programme and effective communication from HR and line managers.
2. Solid empathy baseline
The average empathy index of 69% suggests that most employees can recognise biased situations and consider more than one perspective before acting.
3. Positive response to scenario learning
Qualitative comments (not shown in this mock-up) indicate that staff value the ability to “swap perspectives” and understand how their decisions land on others.
Priority Risks & Gaps
1. Uneven empathy scores across teams
The Engineering and Trading & Markets teams show lower median empathy scores than Operations. This may point to differences in local leadership culture or workload pressures.
2. Allyship behaviours lag behind awareness
While recognition of bias is relatively strong, allyship behaviours (e.g., speaking up, supporting colleagues, challenging unfair decisions) are less frequent in the scenarios.
3. Psychological safety is inconsistent
Employees are more likely to choose “avoidant” options in scenarios where power dynamics are strong (for example, challenging a senior leader or a long-standing team norm).
Recommended Actions (Prototype)
Short term (0–3 months)
• Use scenario content in existing team meetings and away days to normalise conversations about bias and inclusion.
• Encourage managers to share their own learning journeys and “mistakes” to model growth mindsets.
• Identify 2–3 “quick win” policy or process changes that address common patterns from scenarios (for example, structured interview questions or clearer meeting norms).
Medium term (3–12 months)
• Develop a set of allyship micro-skills (e.g., asking clarifying questions, naming behaviour, inviting quieter voices) and embed them into performance conversations.
• Extend the Trading Places scenario library to cover issues such as disability inclusion, hybrid working, and cross-cultural collaboration.
Long term (12+ months)
• Integrate culture-intelligence metrics into organisational dashboards alongside operational KPIs.
• Periodically refresh scenarios to reflect evolving legal, social, and organisational contexts.
Legal & Ethical Considerations
This prototype is designed with reference to GDPR, the Equality Act 2010, and the Public Sector Equality Duty. It uses mock, anonymised data only and does not collect or process real employee records.
In a production deployment, the organisation would need to complete a full Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA), update privacy notices, and ensure that scenario content and analytics do not disadvantage protected groups.
