Coaching Suggestions
  • 1. Use scenarios in 1:1s
    Invite team members to discuss what surprised them in a scenario rather than focusing on their score. This keeps the conversation developmental, not evaluative.
  • 2. Model vulnerability
    Share a moment where your own assumptions were challenged. This signals that learning is expected, not punished, and supports psychological safety.
  • 3. Watch for patterns
    Look at which types of choices (voice, challenge, allyship) your team tends to avoid and explore why in team discussions.
Focus Areas for Your Team
  • Allyship behaviours
    Team members score well on recognising bias but are less likely to choose active allyship responses (e.g. speaking up, backing a colleague).
  • Challenging up
    In scenarios involving senior leaders, “avoidant” options are chosen more often. Consider how you can make it safer to challenge decisions.
  • Onboarding & early experiences
    First-day and early-career scenarios suggest some colleagues feel unsure about “fitting in”. Use onboarding and buddying to address this.
